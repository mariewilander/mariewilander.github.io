Anne Boleyn was the Queen of England from 1533 to 1536.

Henry VIII attempted to seduce her and she refused to become his mistress.

Henry attempted to annull his marriage to Catherine, and the Catholic Church said no way.

Henry and his advisers started to close monasteries and had a secret wedding with Anne.

Five days after the new Archbishop of Canterbury declared Henry's marriage with Catherine null and void, he declared his marriage with Anne was valid.

Anne gave birth to the who would be the future Queen Elizabeth I, and unfortunately suffered from three miscarriages while Henry was courting Jane Seymour.

In April 1536, Henry had Anne investigated for high treason. In May, she was arrested and taken to the Tower of London.

She was convicted on May 15th and beheaded four days later. The charges included adultery, incest, and plotting to kill the king, which historians dispute.

When Anne's daughter, Elizabeth became queen in 1558, Anne became a martyr. She's remembered as one of the most influential and important queens England ever had.
